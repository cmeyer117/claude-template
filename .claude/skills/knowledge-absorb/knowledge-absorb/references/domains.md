# Domain Standards — Knowledge Absorb Skill

Domain-specific guidance for expert-level teaching in Carl's core areas.

---

## 💪 Bodybuilding / Physique / Competition Prep

**Gold standard sources:** Brad Schoenfeld (hypertrophy research), Eric Helms (evidence-based dieting),
Mike Israetel (RP Strength — volume landmarks), Lyle McDonald (nutrition science), 
Layne Norton (peri-workout nutrition, powerbuilding), Paul Carter (training philosophy),
Ben Pakulski (MI40 — mind-muscle connection).

**Key concepts to always know cold:**
- Mechanotensive overload vs. metabolic stress
- MEV / MAV / MRV framework (Israetel)
- Protein synthesis windows and leucine threshold
- Periodization: linear, undulating, block
- Contest prep: peak week, water/carb manipulation
- Drug-enhanced vs. natural physiology distinctions (teach honestly)

**Teaching standard:** Don't soften anything. The bodybuilding world is full of bro-science.
Always distinguish what is mechanistically proven vs. practically reported.
When a principle sounds good but lacks RCT support, say so.

## 📊 Accounting / CPA / Tax

**Gold standard sources:** AICPA standards, IRS publications, Becker CPA review,
Roger CPA review, Jeff Elliott (Another71), Randy Brooks.
Academic: Kieso/Weygandt (Intermediate Accounting), Hoffman/Smith (Tax),
Rittenberg (Auditing).

**Key domains:**
- FAR: Financial reporting, GAAP, IFRS
- REG: Tax (individual, corporate, partnerships), business law
- AUD: Auditing standards, PCAOB, risk assessment
- BEC: Business environment, economics, IT controls, COSO

**Teaching standard:** Always connect theory to practice. Abstract accounting rules
become sticky when you connect them to real transactions Carl will encounter in the firm.
E.g., don't just define "basis" — show what happens at sale, at death (step-up), at gift.

## 📱 Content / Personal Brand / Social Media

**Gold standard sources:** Alex Hormozi (offers, sales, content strategy),
Gary Vaynerchuk (attention arbitrage, platform strategy), Creator Economy research (ConvertKit,
Patreon, Influencer Marketing Hub reports), Nicholas Cole (ghostwriting/content frameworks),
Chris Do (design + business + creative direction), MrBeast (retention mechanics — YouTube).

**Key concepts:**
- Hook architecture (first 3 seconds)
- Value ladder: free → low-ticket → high-ticket
- Niche authority vs. broad appeal tradeoffs
- Platform algorithm differences (TikTok = discovery, IG = retention, YouTube = depth)
- Brand deal valuation: CPM, engagement rate, exclusivity windows

**Teaching standard:** Always connect to Carl's specific lanes — bodybuilding content,
streamer brand deals, accounting firm positioning. Abstract content strategy is useless;
show how it maps to his actual audience and goals.

## 🤖 Software / AI / Automation

**Gold standard sources:** Andrej Karpathy (deep learning fundamentals),
Sam Altman / Dario Amodei (AI trajectory), Andrew Ng (ML frameworks),
Lenny Rachitsky (product strategy), Paul Graham (startups/thinking),
Swyx / Latent Space (AI engineering community), Harrison Chase (LangChain),
n8n/Zapier docs, Vapi documentation.

**Key concepts for Carl's stack:**
- Agentic AI architecture: orchestrator → subagents → tools
- RAG (retrieval-augmented generation) vs. fine-tuning tradeoffs
- Voice AI: STT → LLM → TTS pipeline (Vapi use case)
- Memory systems: short-term (context), long-term (Mem0, vector DB)
- n8n automation: trigger → logic → action
- Accounting automation: OCR + classification + posting pipeline

**Teaching standard:** Connect everything to what Carl is actually building (Jarvis, 
firm automation, coaching dashboard). Avoid academic abstraction without a "here's
how this applies to your system" anchor.

## 🙏 Faith / Mindset / Character / Psychology

**Gold standard sources:** Jordan Peterson (psychological archetypes, responsibility),
Viktor Frankl (meaning, suffering), C.S. Lewis (faith, reason), Stoics — Marcus Aurelius,
Epictetus, Seneca (control, virtue), Dale Carnegie (human relations), Robert Cialdini
(influence, persuasion), Carol Dweck (growth mindset), Ryan Holiday (Stoicism applied),
James Clear (habit formation), Daniel Kahneman (cognitive bias).

**Key concepts:**
- Locus of control (Rotter) — internal vs. external
- Cognitive reframing (CBT root)
- Identity-based habit formation (Clear)
- Logos / meaning-making under suffering (Frankl)
- Shadow integration (Jung / Peterson)
- Deep work vs. shallow work (Newport)
- Persuasion principles: reciprocity, social proof, authority, scarcity (Cialdini)

**Teaching standard:** Carl puts God first. When teaching mindset and philosophy,
honor that lens — don't push secular frameworks as the only frame. Show how secular
psychology often rediscovers what spiritual traditions already knew.
Challenge Carl's thinking, but respect the foundation.

## 💰 Finances / Investing / Business

**Gold standard sources:** Benjamin Graham / Warren Buffett (value investing),
Charlie Munger (mental models, latticework), Morgan Housel (psychology of money),
Nassim Taleb (antifragility, risk), Ray Dalio (principles, macro),
Alex Hormozi ($100M Offers, Leads), MJ DeMarco (Millionaire Fastlane — fastlane thinking),
Robert Kiyosaki (cash flow quadrant — conceptual, not dogma).

**Key concepts:**
- Cash flow vs. capital gains
- Compounding (mathematical + behavioral)
- Risk-adjusted return (Sharpe ratio conceptually)
- Business valuation: multiples, DCF basics
- Tax-advantaged accounts: Solo 401k (relevant for self-employed CPA),
  S-corp vs. LLC taxation, QBI deduction
- Mental models: opportunity cost, sunk cost, margin of safety

**Teaching standard:** Carl is building wealth on multiple fronts simultaneously
(accounting firm, online coaching, content, streamer deals). Always connect
principles to his actual cash flow situation — not abstract "invest in index funds"
generic advice.
