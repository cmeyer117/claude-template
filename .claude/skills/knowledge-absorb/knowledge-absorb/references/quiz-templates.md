# Quiz Templates — Knowledge Absorb Skill

Use these question formats to build retention quizzes in Quiz Mode.
Always mix difficulty levels. Label each question with its level.

---

## Level 1: Recall
Tests whether the user can remember the basic idea.
"What does [author] call the principle where..."
"Name three of the key [concepts] from [source]."
"What's the one-sentence core thesis of [book]?"

## Level 2: Comprehension
Tests whether they understand it, not just remember it.
"Why does [author] argue that X leads to Y?"
"Explain the difference between [concept A] and [concept B] as [author] defines them."
"What problem is [principle] solving?"

## Level 3: Application
Tests whether they can use it.
"You're pitching a brand deal to Gymshark for one of your streamers. Which principle from [book] is most relevant and how would you apply it?"
"You're onboarding a new accounting client who is skeptical of your firm. What would [author] say to do first?"
"You're 8 weeks out from a competition and motivation is dropping. What principle from [book] applies here?"

## Level 4: Synthesis
Tests whether they can connect ideas across sources.
"[Author A] says X. [Author B] says Y. Who's right and why?"
"How does [principle from Book 1] contradict or support [principle from Book 2]?"
"What's the underlying idea both [thinker A] and [thinker B] are pointing at, even though they describe it differently?"

## Level 5: Challenge (Stress Test)
Forces genuine critical thinking.
"What's the strongest argument AGAINST this author's main claim?"
"Where has this principle failed in the real world? Give an example."
"If this principle is true, what uncomfortable implication does it have for [specific life lane]?"

---

## Quiz Formatting

Present questions numbered. After the user answers (in a follow-up message),
grade them and give feedback:
- ✅ Correct / strong answer — reinforce with why it's right
- 🟡 Partially right — sharpen what they missed
- ❌ Off — redirect without judgment, explain the correct framing
